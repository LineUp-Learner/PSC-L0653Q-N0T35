# Hierarchical Classification of Executive Instruments and Governance Concepts

## Hierarchical Structure

Here is a hierarchical organization of governance concepts from broadest to most specific:

- **Policy** (Broadest level - sets vision and direction)
    - **Strategy** (Operationalizes policy vision. Identify the approaches)
        - **Framework** (Structures the best implementation approach)
            - **Mission** (Focused, time-bound implementation of policy priorities)
                - **Program** (Collection of related projects)
                    - **Scheme** (Targeted intervention mechanism)
                        - **Project** (Specific implementation unit)
                            - **Initiative** (Specific activities or interventions)

1. **Legislation/Act** (Parallel legal framework)
    - **Regulation** (Detailed rules under legislation)

## Comprehensive & Integrated (levels based) Examples

### **_India's Climate Action_**

- #### Policy Level
    - **Policy**: National Policy on Climate Change
- #### Framework & Strategy Level
    - **Strategy**: National Strategy on Climate Change Adaptation
        - **Framework**: National Framework for Climate Resilient Development
- #### Implementation Level
    - **Mission**: National Solar Mission
        - **Program**: National Adaptation Program of Action
            - **Scheme**: PM-KUSUM (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan)
                - **Project**: Namami Gange River Cleaning Project
                    - **Initiative**: Climate Smart Cities Initiative
- #### Legislation Level
    - **Legislation**: Energy Conservation Act (Amended)
        - **Regulation**: Carbon Credit Trading Regulations

### **_Water Management in India_**

- #### Policy Level
    - **Policy:** National Water Policy (sets broad vision for water management)
- #### Framework & Strategy Level
    - **Strategy:** National Water Management Strategy (approaches for implementing the policy)
        - **Framework:** Integrated Water Resources Management Framework (structure for implementation)
- #### Implementation Level
    - **Mission:** National Water Mission (time-bound mission to address water security)
        - **Program:** National River Conservation Program (coordinated set of river projects)
            - **Scheme:** Jal Jeevan Mission (targeted scheme for providing tap water to households)
                - **Project:** Yamuna Action Plan (specific project to clean the Yamuna river)
                    - **Initiative:** Rainwater Harvesting Initiative (specific intervention to promote rainwater collection
- #### Legislation Level
    - **Legislation:** Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974
        - **Regulation:** Central Ground Water Authority Regulations (specific rules for groundwater extraction)

## Detailed Definitions and Differentiations

### 1. Policy

**Definition:** A policy is a high-level statement of intent, principles, and goals that guides decision-making to achieve rational outcomes. It represents the broadest level of governance direction.

**Key characteristics:**

- Provides long-term vision and direction
- Establishes broad principles and priorities
- Usually formulated by the highest level of government
- Influences all subordinate instruments

**Example:** National Education Policy 2020

### 2. Strategy

**Definition:** A strategy is a carefully devised plan of action to achieve the policy objectives, detailing the approach and methods to be employed.

**Key characteristics:**

- Translates policy into actionable approaches
- Usually medium to long-term in timeframe
- Identifies key focus areas and methodologies
- More specific than policy but broader than programs

**Example:** National Strategy for Financial Education

### 3. Framework

**Definition:** A framework provides a structured approach or system that organizes how policy objectives will be implemented, often defining boundaries, principles, and general methods.

**Key characteristics:**

- Creates structure for implementation
- Defines relationships between components
- Establishes guidelines and boundaries
- Provides consistency across implementation

**Example:** National Digital Health Blueprint

### 4. Mission

**Definition:** A mission is a focused, large-scale, time-bound initiative designed to achieve specific policy objectives through coordinated action across multiple schemes and projects.

**Key characteristics:**

- Has clear, measurable objectives
- Set timeframe for completion
- Usually higher profile with dedicated leadership
- Addresses a specific policy priority
- Often operates across multiple domains

**Example:** National Solar Mission

### 5. Program

**Definition:** A program is a coordinated group of related projects managed in a coordinated way to obtain benefits not available from managing them individually.

**Key characteristics:**

- Consists of multiple related projects
- Medium to long-term duration
- Broader in scope than individual projects
- Has multiple components working toward common goals

**Example:** Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) Program

### 6. Scheme

**Definition:** A scheme is a systematically organized plan that provides a specific mechanism to address targeted objectives, often with defined eligibility criteria and benefits.

**Key characteristics:**

- Has specific eligibility criteria and benefits
- Detailed implementation guidelines
- Usually has dedicated funding mechanism
- More specific and operational than programs
- Often directly interfaces with beneficiaries

**Example:** Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana

### 7. Project

**Definition:** A project is a temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result with a defined beginning and end.

**Key characteristics:**

- Has specific objectives, timeline, and deliverables
- Defined resources and budget
- Usually has a dedicated project manager
- Most specific level of planned intervention

**Example:** Delhi Metro Rail Project

### 8. Initiative

**Definition:** An initiative is a specific action, activity, or intervention designed to address a particular aspect of a larger scheme or project.

**Key characteristics:**

- Usually smaller in scale
- Often a component of a larger project or scheme
- More flexible and adaptable
- Can be experimental or pilot in nature

**Example:** Digital Saksharta Abhiyan (computer literacy initiative)

### 9. Legislation/Act

**Definition:** A legislation or act is a law enacted by a legislative body, establishing legal frameworks, rights, and obligations with the force of law.

**Key characteristics:**

- Created through formal legislative process
- Has legal enforceability
- Establishes rights and obligations
- Creates legal authority for implementation
- Exists parallel to administrative instruments

**Example:** Right to Education Act, 2009

### 10. Regulation

**Definition:** Regulations are specific rules issued by government agencies under the authority of legislation to implement, interpret, or prescribe law or policy.

**Key characteristics:**

- More detailed than legislation
- Issued by executive agencies
- Provides operational details
- Easier to modify than legislation
- Derives authority from parent legislation

**Example:** SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India) Regulations

## Additional Related Concepts

### 11. Plan

**Definition:** A plan is a proposed course of action detailing what needs to be done, when, how, and by whom to achieve specific objectives.
**Example:** Five-Year Plans (India's historical economic planning approach)

### 12. Campaign

**Definition:** A campaign is an organized course of actions for a specific purpose, usually time-limited and focused on raising awareness or changing behaviour.
**Example:** Swachh Bharat Abhiyan

### 13. Protocol

**Definition:** A protocol is a set of formal rules, guidelines, or standards governing specific processes or interactions.
**Example:** Disaster Management Protocol

### 14. Guideline

**Definition:** Guidelines are recommended courses of action that provide direction but may not be mandatory like regulations.
**Example:** ICMR Guidelines for COVID-19 Management

### 15. Agenda

**Definition:** An agenda is a list of issues or items to be addressed by policy or governance institutions.
**Example:** Sustainable Development Agenda 2030

The #Objectives_Resolution was a pivotal document in the creation of the Indian #constitution . It was moved by Jawaharlal #Nehru in the #Constituent_Assembly on December 13, 1946, and adopted on January 22, 1947. This resolution outlined the philosophy and vision that guided the drafting of the Constitution.

Here's a breakdown of the key elements of the Objectives Resolution:

**1. India as an Independent Sovereign Republic:**

- The resolution declared India to be an "Independent #Sovereign #Republic ," signifying its complete freedom from foreign control and the ultimate authority residing in its people.

**2. Justice:**

- It envisioned securing " #Justice , social, economic and political" for all citizens, encompassing:
    - #Social_Justice : Addressing social inequalities and ensuring equal opportunities regardless of caste, creed, or social standing.
    - #Economic_Justice : Promoting a fair distribution of wealth and resources, preventing exploitation.
    - #Political_Justice : Guaranteeing equal #political_rights , including voting, participation in government, and holding public office.

**3. Liberty:**

- The resolution aimed to secure " #Liberty of thought, expression, belief, faith and worship," which meant:
    - #Freedom of thought and expression: The right to voice opinions and views freely.
    - #Freedom of belief, faith, and worship: The right to practice and profess any religion or belief.

**4. Equality:**

- It sought to secure " #Equality of status and of opportunity," implying:
    - #Equality of status: Abolishing titles and ensuring equal treatment before the law.
    - #Equality of opportunity: Providing equal opportunities in education, employment, and other aspects of life.

**5. Fraternity:**

- The resolution emphasized " #Fraternity assuring the dignity of the individual and the #unity of the Nation," which meant:
    - Promoting a sense of #brotherhood and sisterhood among all citizens.
    - Respecting the dignity of every individual, irrespective of background.
    - Ensuring the #unity and #integrity of the nation.

**6. Fundamental Rights:**

- The resolution stressed the need to guarantee " #fundamental_right" to all citizens, which are basic rights essential for human dignity and development.

**7. Safeguards for Minorities, Backward Classes, and Tribal Areas:**

- It committed to providing "adequate safeguards" for #minorities , #backward_classes , and tribal areas, recognizing their special needs and aiming to protect their interests.

**8. Territorial Integrity:**

- The resolution affirmed the resolve to maintain the " #territorial_integrity " of India and its #Sovereign rights, emphasizing the importance of preserving India's borders and protecting its #sovereignty .

**9. Contribution to World Peace:**

- It expressed India's desire to contribute to the promotion of " #world_peace ," reflecting its commitment to peaceful #coexistence and #international_cooperation ( #cooperation ) .

**Significance:**

- **Philosophical Foundation:** The Objectives Resolution provided the philosophical #foundation and direction for drafting the Indian #constitution.
- **Aspirations of the People:** It captured the hopes and aspirations of the Indian people for a free, just, and #egalitarian society.
- **Consensus Building:** It helped build #consensus among the diverse members of the #Constituent_Assembly on the fundamental goals of the Constitution.
- **Inspiration for the Preamble:** The #Preamble of the Indian Constitution, which encapsulates the aims and objectives of the Constitution, is largely inspired by the #Objectives_Resolution .

In essence, the Objectives Resolution was a landmark document that articulated the vision of a new India and provided the moral and philosophical compass for the Constituent Assembly. It laid the groundwork for a Constitution that would guarantee justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity to all its citizens and contribute to a peaceful world.

# Case Study: #Community Participation and Indigenous Welfare Practices

### **Halma Practices of #Bhil Community, #Jhabua (MP), #Shiv-ganga Movement, #Padma_Shri Mahesh Sharma**

---

#### **0. Context Building & Prerequisites**

- **Key Concepts**:
    - **Bhil Tribe**: Largest #tribal group in India (8.6 million, Census 2011), primarily in #MP , #Gujarat , #Rajasthan .
    - **Halma**: Collective labour system rooted in Bhil culture, emphasizing mutual aid and resource-sharing.
    - **Shiv-ganga Movement**: Grassroots ecological initiative in #Jhabua focusing on #water_conservation , #Water_management and agroforestry.
    - **Padma Shri Mahesh Sharma**: Social activist from Jhabua, instrumental in reviving Halma and linking it to sustainable development.

---

#### **1. What**

- **Halma**:
    - **Definition**: Traditional practice where #Bhil community members pool labour for farming, #water_conservation , or #infrastructure projects.
    - **Global Analogues**:
        - _Japan’s “Yui”_ (mutual aid in Okinawa).
        - _Kenya’s “Harambee”_ (community self-help).
    - **Local Classification**: #Tribal #cooperative system under #Article_244 (Fifth Schedule) for tribal #self-governance .
- **Shiv-ganga Movement**:
    - Integrates #Halma with #water*harvesting (e.g., \_check dams, farm ponds*) and #organic_farming.
    - Led by Bhil women, emphasizing #gender_empowerment .
- **Padma Shri Mahesh Sharma**:
    - Revived Halma through #NGO like _Narmada Samagra_; linked it to #NREGA for scalability.

---

#### **2. Salient Features**

- **Halma**:
    - Non-monetary labour exchange.
    - #Decentralized #decision-making by #Panch - the #village_council
    - Intergenerational knowledge transfer.
- #Shiv-ganga **Movement** :
    - Combines traditional practices with modern #watershed_management .
    - Created 500+ water structures in Jhabua (2010–2020).

---

#### **3. When & Where**

- **Halma**:
    - **Temporal**: Practiced since #pre-colonial era; revived post-2000 due to #climate stress.
    - **Spatial**: Concentrated in Jhabua, Alirajpur ( #MP ), and Dahod ( #Gujarat ).
- **Shiv-ganga Movement**: Active since 2005 in Jhabua’s 50+ villages.

---

#### **4. Why & How**

- **Causes**:
    - **Ecological**: 70% rainfall decline in Jhabua (1990–2020, #IMD data).
    - **Socio-Economic**: Poverty (45% ST population below poverty line, #NITI Aayog 2021).
- **Process**:
    - #Halma workdays organized during off-farm seasons.
    - Use of #Doha (traditional seed balls) for #afforestation .
- **Impact**:
    - **Environmental**: 20% rise in #groundwater levels (Jhabua, 2015–2020).
    - **Social**: Reduced male emigration by 15% ( #UNICEF 2019).

---

#### **5. Who**

- **Stakeholders**:
    - **Initiators**: Bhil elders and women’s self-help groups ( #SHG ).
    - **Propagators**: #Mahesh_Sharma (bridged Halma with #NREGA ).
    - **Influencers**: NGOs (Aga Khan Foundation), state #tribal department.
    - **Affected**: 200,000+ #Bhils and #Bhilala in Jhabua.

---

#### **6. Historical Context**

- **Pre-Independence**: Halma as resistance against colonial #forest laws (e.g., #Indian_Forest_Act , 1927).
- **Post-1990s**: Decline due to #industrialization ; revival post-2005 via eco-movements.

---

#### **7. Challenges**

- **Legal**: Ambiguity in #Panchayats (Extension to #Scheduled_Areas ) Act, 1996 implementation.
- **Economic**: Limited #NREGA convergence (only 30% Halma projects linked).
- **Cultural**: Erosion of tribal identity among #youth.
- **Environmental**: #soil_salinity from excessive borewell use.

---

#### **8. Initiatives & Policies**

- **Government**:
    - **MP’s #Jan_Abhiyan_Parishad**: Funds #SHG for Halma-based projects.
    - #National_Water_Mission : Aligns with #Shiv-ganga ’s #watershed work.
- **Non-Govt**:
    - #Shiv-ganga Trust: Trained 10,000+ farmers in organic techniques.

---

#### **9. Public Administration**

- **District #Collector , Jhabua**: Facilitated Halma-NREGA convergence (e.g., #Hariyali model).
- **MP #Tribal_Welfare_Department**: Allocated ₹50 crore (2021–22) for tribal land development.

---

#### **10. Implementation & Effects**

- **Output**: 1,200+ #check_dams built via #Halma (2010–2022).
- **Outcome**: 40% increase in #millet production (Jhabua, 2022).

---

#### **11. Value Addition**

- **Data**: Jhabua’s #forest cover increased by 12% (2011–2021, FSI).
- **SDGs**: #SDG_15 (Life on Land), #SDG_5 ( #Gender_equality ).
- **Reports**: #UNDP ’s “Journey of Halma”\* (2020).
- **Judgements**: _Samatha v. Andhra Pradesh (1997)_: Reinforced tribal land rights.

---

#### **12. Comparative Analysis**

- **Spatial**:
    - _India_: Halma vs. Kerala’s #Pattom_Chittas - #land_cooperatives.
    - _Global_: Halma vs. Peru’s #Minka ( #Inca communal labour).
- **Temporal**:
    - Pre-2000 Halma (subsistence focus) vs. Post-2000 ( #climate_resilience).

---

#### **13. Best Practices**

- **Global**: Israel’s #drip_irrigation + #community management .
- **India**: Halma + #MGNREGA in Jhabua (2.5 lakh workdays/year).

---

#### **14. Adaptation Strategies**

#### **15. Applications & Implications**

- **Implications**: Strengthened #tribal agency under #PESA Act.

---

#### **16. Future Outlook**

#### **18. Case Studies**

---

#### **19. Conclusion**

Halma exemplifies how tribal wisdom can address modern challenges like climate change and rural poverty. Its institutionalization through policies like #MGNREGA and advocacy by figures like Mahesh Sharma offers a blueprint for #sustainable_development.

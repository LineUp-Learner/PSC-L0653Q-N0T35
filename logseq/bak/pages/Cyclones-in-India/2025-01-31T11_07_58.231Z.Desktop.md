## 1. What - Definition & Classification
- ### Definition (IMD Official)
  
  Cyclone: An intense vortex of winds spiralling clockwise in Southern Hemisphere and counter-clockwise in Northern Hemisphere, characterized by rapid pressure drop (≥ 5-6 hPa/100 km.sq) and wind speed >= 74 kmph.
- ### Classifications
  
  1. Based on Formation Location:
	- #bay_of_bengal Cyclones (More frequent - 80%)
	- #arabian_sea Cyclones (Less frequent - 20%)
	  
	  2. #IMD Classification (Speed-based):
	- #Depression : 31-49 kmph
	- Deep Depression: 50-61 kmph
	- Cyclonic Storm: 62-88 kmph
	- Severe Cyclonic Storm: 89-117 kmph
	- Very Severe Cyclonic Storm: 118-166 kmph
	- Extremely Severe Cyclonic Storm: 167-221 kmph
	- Super Cyclonic Storm : >222 kmph
	  
	  3. #saffir_simpson_scale (International):
	- Category 1: 119-153 kmph
	- Category 2: 154-177 kmph
	- Category 3: 178-208 kmph
	- Category 4: 209-251 kmph
	- Category 5: >252 kmph

## 3. Why & How - Causation & Impact Analysis
- ### Causative Factors
  
  1. Primary Factors:
	- #sea_surface_temperature ≥ 26.5°C
	- #coriolis_force > 0.5×10⁻⁵ per second
	- Vertical wind shear < 10 m/s
	- Relative #humidity > 50% at mid-troposphere
	- #Ocean_thermal_energy > 50 kJ/cm²
	  
	  2. Secondary Factors:
	- Low-level positive vorticity
	- Upper-level #divergence
	- Mid-tropospheric humidity
	- Convective instability #convection
	- Ocean heat content
- ### Process Analysis
  
  1. Formation Stage:
	- Initial #disturbance development
	- Moisture convergence
	- Vertical motion establishment
	- Duration: 24-48 hours
	  
	  2. Intensification Stage:
	- Pressure gradient strengthening
	- Wind field organization
	- #Eye_formation
	- Duration: 48-72 hours
	  
	  3. Mature Stage:
	- Maximum wind speeds
	- Minimum central pressure
	- Well-defined eye
	- Duration: 24-36 hours
	  
	  4. Decay Stage:
	- #Landfall effects
	- Energy dissipation
	- System filling
	- Duration: 12-24 hours
